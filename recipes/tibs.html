Tibst

Spiced Sautéed Meat (A Celebration Dish)

Tibs (or T'ibs) is one of the most popular and quickly prepared dishes in Ethiopian cuisine, often served during celebrations or as a mark of respect for a guest.

The word "tibs" literally means "fried" or "sautéed." It features bite-sized pieces of meat—most commonly beef or lamb—that are quickly pan-fried with Ethiopian spiced butter, aromatics, and the signature Berbere spice blend.

Ingredients

  • Cubed beef
  • Red Onions(lots)
  • Niter kibbeh
  • Oil
  • Awaze
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Prep: Cut the meat into bite-sized cubes and season lightly with salt.
  2. In a hot pan or skillet, quickly sear the meat (often in batches) until browned, then set it aside.
  3. Melt the Niter Kibbeh (or heat the oil) and quickly sauté the sliced onions, garlic, and ginger.
  4. Return the meat to the pan. Stir in the Berbere spice (or Awaze paste), along with any other seasonings or a splash of water/broth to create a light glaze.
  5. Finish & Serve: Sauté until the meat is cooked to the desired doneness. Serve immediately while still sizzling, often garnished with fresh jalapeño, alongside Injera (the spongy flatbread).
