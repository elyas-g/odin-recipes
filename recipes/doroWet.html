Doro Wot

Ethiopia's National Dish: Rich, Spicy, and Aromatic Chicken Stew

Doro Wet (or Doro Wat) is a celebrated Ethiopian stew made with chicken and hard-boiled eggs.

Its characteristic deep red color and bold flavor come from two essential Ethiopian staples: large quantities of slow-cooked onions that form the rich, thick base, and Berbereâ€”a potent, complex spice blend. The chicken is cooked until incredibly tender and is traditionally served with Injera (a sour, spongy flatbread) to scoop up the luxurious sauce

Ingredients

  • Chicken pieces
  • hard-boiled eggs
  • Red Onions(lots)
  • Niter kibbeh
  • Oil
  • Berbere
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Prep: Wash/marinate the chicken in a lemon/water solution; hard-boil and peel the eggs.
  2. Cook Onions (The Long Step): Dry cook the finely chopped onions in a pot over low heat for 15-30 minutes, stirring until they fully break down into a thick, wet mash. This forms the stew's base.
  3. Build Flavor: Add the Niter Kibbeh (or oil), then stir in the garlic, ginger, and generous amounts of Berbere spice mix. Cook for a few minutes until fragrant.
  4. Simmer: Add water/stock, bring to a boil, then add the chicken. Reduce the heat and simmer gently for 40-60 minutes until the chicken is tender and the sauce is rich and thick.
  5. Finish: Stir in the hard-boiled eggs and simmer for a final 10 minutes to allow them to absorb the spice. Serve hot with Injera.
