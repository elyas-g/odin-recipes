Shiro Wot

Velvety, Savory Chickpea Flour Stew (Vegan-Friendly Staple)

Shiro Wet is a comforting, easy-to-make Ethiopian stew that is a staple, especially during fasting seasons when meat and dairy are avoided.

Its unique, thick, and velvety texture comes from its main ingredient: Shiro Powder (finely ground chickpeas or broad beans, often pre-mixed with spices). The powder is slowly simmered with aromatics like onion, garlic, and ginger, resulting in a rich, flavorful, and protein-packed stew.

Ingredients

  • Shiro powder
  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Niter kibbeh
  • Berbere
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Sauté finely chopped onions in oil (or Niter Kibbeh) until softened and slightly golden. Add garlic, ginger, and tomato/tomato paste, cooking until the mixture reduces.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk the Shiro Powder with water until a lump-free, thick slurry is formed.
  3. Pour the Shiro slurry into the pot with the sautéed aromatics.
  4. Stir continuously over medium heat until the stew comes to a boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for about 5–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a desired, thick consistency and the "raw" flour taste has cooked out
  5. Serve hot, traditionally with Injera.
